# 📌 GROUP TASK 1  
## Contribution of Alan Turing to Artificial Intelligence (AI)

### 📖 Introduction
Alan Mathison Turing (1912–1954) is known as the father of Artificial Intelligence and theoretical computer science. His work transformed the concept of intelligent machines into a scientific discipline.

---

### 🔹 1. Turing Machine (1936)
- Introduced in the paper *On Computable Numbers*.
- A theoretical model capable of executing any algorithm.
- Proved logical processes can be automated.

**Importance to AI:**
- Foundation for AI algorithms
- Basis for programming languages
- Core of intelligent systems

---

### 🔹 2. Universal Computation Concept
- One machine can simulate all other machines.
- Led to modern stored-program computer architecture.

---

### 🔹 3. Turing Test (1950)
- Introduced in *Computing Machinery and Intelligence*.
- Defined intelligence based on machine behavior.
- Replaced the question: “Can machines think?”

---

### 🔹 4. Early Vision of Machine Learning
- Proposed the concept of a “Child Machine”.
- Suggested machines should learn from experience.

---

### 🔹 5. Learning Through Reward
- Early concept of reinforcement learning.
- Used in robotics, game AI, autonomous vehicles.

---

### 🔹 6. Influence on Modern AI
- Recommendation systems
- Autonomous vehicles
- Intelligent assistants

---

### ✅ Conclusion
Alan Turing laid the intellectual foundation of Artificial Intelligence. His work remains deeply embedded in modern AI research and development.

# 📌 GROUP TASK 3  
## ML Ethics & Bias Case Study

### 🎯 Objective
To analyze real-world bias in deployed machine learning systems and identify causes of algorithmic unfairness.

---

### 🔹 Case Study: Bias in Automated Hiring Systems

AI-based resume screening tools:
- Use supervised learning
- Rank and shortlist candidates
- Process textual resume data

---

### 🔹 Observed Bias
- Favored historically dominant demographics
- Penalized gender-indicative keywords

---

### 🔹 Root Causes of Bias

**1. Data-Level Bias**
- Historical hiring bias
- Class imbalance

**2. Feature-Level Bias**
- Gender-correlated text embeddings
- Accuracy-only optimization

---

### 🔹 Ethical Implications
- Violation of fairness principles
- Discriminatory hiring outcomes
- Legal risks

---

### 🔹 Bias Detection Techniques
- Demographic parity difference
- Equal opportunity gap
- SHAP / LIME explainability tools

---

### 🔹 Bias Mitigation Strategies

**Pre-processing:**
- Dataset rebalancing
- Removing sensitive attributes

**In-processing:**
- Fairness-aware loss functions
- Adversarial debiasing

---

### 🔹 Governance Guidelines
- Fairness audits
- Model documentation
- Human-in-the-loop decision systems

---

### 🔹 Trade-offs
- Fairness vs accuracy balance
- Defining universal fairness metrics

---

### ✅ Conclusion
Ethical AI development requires fairness, transparency, and accountability. Responsible AI practices improve trust and societal acceptance.
